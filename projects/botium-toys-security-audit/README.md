# Mock Security Audit: Botium Toys

## Overview

This project was completed as part of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate.

The activity involved carrying out a mock internal security audit for a fictional company called Botium Toys. The purpose of the exercise was to review the organisation’s security posture, assess risk, identify gaps in controls and consider relevant compliance requirements.

This was a course-based scenario, but I approached it as a practical exercise in understanding how security audits help organisations identify weaknesses, prioritise improvements and reduce risk.

---

## Source / Context

The audit was based on a fictional company scenario provided as part of the course, including a scope, goals, risk assessment report and controls checklist.

This write-up summarises my own analysis and recommendations in my own words for portfolio purposes.

## Audit Objectives

The main objectives of the audit were to:

- Identify existing security controls
- Highlight missing or weak controls
- Review potential risks to business operations and customer data
- Assess whether the organisation was meeting relevant compliance expectations
- Recommend improvements to strengthen the company’s security posture

---

## Areas Reviewed

The audit considered several key areas of security, including:

- Asset management
- Access control
- Password and account management
- Network security
- Data protection
- Backup and recovery
- Incident response readiness
- Physical security
- Compliance requirements

---

## Key Findings

The audit identified several areas where security could be improved.

Examples included:

- Lack of clear access control and least privilege practices
- Weaknesses around password management and authentication
- Gaps in monitoring and detection capability
- Limited evidence of formal incident response planning
- Incomplete backup and disaster recovery controls
- Potential compliance gaps around handling sensitive customer and payment data

These findings showed that while some controls were in place, the organisation would benefit from a more structured and proactive approach to security.

---

## Risk Summary

The overall risk level was considered high due to the number of missing or incomplete controls across key areas.

The most important risks related to:

- Unauthorised access to systems or data
- Loss or exposure of sensitive customer information
- Lack of visibility into suspicious activity
- Difficulty responding effectively to incidents
- Business disruption from data loss or system failure

---

## Recommended Improvements

Based on the audit findings, recommended improvements included:

- Implementing stronger access control and least privilege principles
- Introducing or improving multi-factor authentication
- Strengthening password policies and account management
- Improving logging, monitoring and alerting
- Creating or updating an incident response plan
- Establishing reliable backup and disaster recovery procedures
- Reviewing compliance requirements around payment and customer data
- Documenting security policies and reviewing them regularly

## What I Learned

This activity helped me understand how security audits are used to assess an organisation’s overall security posture.

It also helped reinforce the importance of:

- Reviewing controls in a structured way
- Thinking about risk in business terms, not just technical terms
- Understanding how missing controls can affect confidentiality, integrity and availability
- Linking security recommendations to real organisational risks
- Documenting findings clearly so they can be understood and acted on

This was a useful introduction to governance, risk and compliance concepts, and helped me see how security analysis is not only about spotting technical issues, but also about understanding how those issues affect the wider organisation.
