# Mock Security Audit: Botium Toys

## Objective

This project was completed as part of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate.

The objective was to complete a mock internal security audit for a fictional company called Botium Toys. The audit involved reviewing the organisation’s security scope, assets, current controls, risk level and compliance posture, then identifying which controls and best practices should be prioritised to improve security.

The purpose of the activity was to practise thinking like a security professional: reviewing risk, identifying control gaps, considering compliance requirements and making practical recommendations that could reduce business risk.

---

## Scenario

Botium Toys is a fictional business with both online and physical retail operations. The scope of the audit covered the full security programme, including employee devices, internal systems, network infrastructure, business applications, data storage, ecommerce, inventory management and legacy systems.

The audit found that Botium Toys had some controls in place, but several important areas needed improvement. The overall risk score was rated as 8 out of 10, mainly due to missing controls, weak asset management and gaps in compliance best practices.

## Audit Scope

The audit covered the wider security programme at Botium Toys, including:

* Employee devices and equipment
* Internal network
* On-premises systems
* Ecommerce and inventory systems
* Accounting, telecommunication, database and security systems
* Internet access
* Data retention and storage
* Legacy system maintenance
* Physical store, office and warehouse assets

---

## Key Findings

The audit identified several areas where Botium Toys needed to improve its security posture.

### Controls already in place

Botium Toys had some useful controls already implemented, including:

* Firewall
* Antivirus software
* Physical locks
* CCTV surveillance
* Fire detection and prevention systems
* Data integrity controls
* Data availability controls
* Breach notification process for EU customers
* Privacy policies and procedures

### Controls needing improvement or implementation

Several important controls were missing, incomplete or weak, including:

* Least privilege
* Separation of duties
* Disaster recovery planning
* Backups for critical data
* Intrusion detection system
* Strong password policies
* Centralised password management
* Encryption for sensitive and payment data
* Scheduled monitoring and maintenance for legacy systems
* Formal access control processes
* Data classification and inventory

---

## Compliance Considerations

The audit considered several compliance areas, including PCI DSS, GDPR and SOC-type controls.

### PCI DSS

Botium Toys handled customer credit card information, but several payment security best practices were not being met. The main concerns were around access to cardholder data, secure storage and processing, encryption, and password management.

### GDPR

Botium Toys had a breach notification process for EU customers and privacy policies in place, but customer data was not being fully protected. Improvements were needed around data security, classification, inventory and access control.

### SOC Type 1 / SOC Type 2

The audit identified gaps around user access policies, confidentiality of sensitive information and availability of data to authorised users. Data integrity controls were in place, but stronger access management and security oversight were needed.

---

## Risk Summary

The overall risk level was high.

The main risks were:

* Unauthorised access to customer or internal data
* Exposure of cardholder data and personally identifiable information
* Lack of visibility into suspicious activity due to no IDS
* Business disruption due to no disaster recovery plan or critical data backups
* Increased risk from weak password policies and no password management system
* Unclear monitoring and maintenance process for legacy systems
* Potential fines or regulatory issues due to compliance gaps

---

## Recommendations

Based on the audit findings, the following improvements should be prioritised:

1. Implement least privilege and separation of duties
2. Strengthen access control policies and user access reviews
3. Improve password policy requirements
4. Introduce a centralised password management system
5. Encrypt sensitive customer and payment data
6. Install and configure an intrusion detection system
7. Create and test a disaster recovery plan
8. Implement regular backups for critical data
9. Establish a clear schedule for legacy system monitoring and maintenance
10. Improve data classification and asset inventory processes
11. Review PCI DSS, GDPR and SOC-related compliance gaps
12. Document and regularly review security policies and procedures

## What I Learned

This activity helped me understand that security audits are not just about technical tools. They also involve policies, processes, asset management, business continuity, compliance and communication with stakeholders.

The biggest takeaway was that missing controls often create linked risks. For example, weak access control, no encryption and poor data classification all increase the risk of sensitive data exposure. Similarly, no backups and no disaster recovery plan create a serious business continuity risk.

This project helped me practise reviewing security posture in a structured way and turning findings into clear recommendations.
